Childhood

      1 was given a not so complicated name Jeleazar. 2 I was born on July 29, 2002, in the charming town of Cabanatuan. 3 My parents worked so hard to support our family. 4 I have 3 siblings, two older sisters and a younger brother. 5 We were raised in a loving and supportive home. 6 I grew up in a very religious household where we attended church every Sunday morning 7 My parents raised us with high morals and values from an early age. 8 I had a strong interest in basketball and played on the squad with my friends. 9 I also enjoyed traveling to see my distant grandparents, who lived in Bulacan. 10 The people who have supported me the most and have always told me to follow my aspirations are my grandparents.11 Video games have been a part of my life since I was a young child. 12 My parents bought our first console, the play station 2, it is a gift for my birthday. 13 We would spend hours playing games like “Space Invaders” and “Asteroids”. 14 As I got older, my tastes in video games changed to more mature titles such as “Grand Theft Auto” and “Red Dead Redemption”. 15 Video games have always been an escape for me from reality and they still are today.16 My early years were full of joy, love, and special occasions. 17 I had the good fortune to grow up in a loving family and close-knit community. 18 I attended elementary school in Cabanatuan and did well academically. 19 Overall, I had a really happy childhood, and I'm grateful for the events that helped me become the person I am now. 20 I it is no wonder that I miss those days.


Teenage

      1 I should start by saying that as I get older and grow older, my body is changing.2 It is mostly for us that the change of preference and consciousness on our looks started in our teenage.3 As a teenager, I continued to focus on my education and not quite excelled but I did well in my studies.4 My high school classmates are always competing with one another, so I have started to develop a motto of my own.5 One shouldn't put too much pressure on themselves to perform well in their exams because, in the end, grades don't matter;6 what matters is the level of information one has.7 Therefore, put more emphasis on learning than on grades.2 Still, learnings and discipline reflect grades.8 I discovered a passion for programming and spent countless hours learning new skills and building projects.9 My parents supported my interests and encouraged me to pursue my passions.10 I started to develop a sense of independence and responsibility, taking on part-time jobs to save money for college.11 I had my first serious relationship during this time, learning about love and heartbreak.12 I struggled with self-doubt and insecurity at times but had a strong support system of family and friends to help me through.13 I started to consider my future plans and career goals, researching universities and exploring potential majors. Of course, I lived my teenage life with fulfillment. 14 While enjoying myself I did not forget how to act in accord with what my parents taught me.15 I had a close group of friends who I spent most of my time with, and we would often have sleepovers and play video games together.16 My family was very close and we would have big gatherings with extended family on holidays and special occasions. 17 There are things that I missed in my teenage time. 18 In my teenage years, I had boundaries that I didn't cross.19 I still assume that there are missed opportunities to live and act like one.20 Careless but not immoral, I supposed as of now I can't act rashly like before.21 Overall, my childhood was filled with love, laughter, and adventure, and I am grateful for the experiences and memories I made.


College Years

      1 The college experience often involves balancing academics, social life, and personal responsibilities. 2 My college years were some of the most formative and exciting times of my life.3 I attended AMA Cabanatuan Campus, where I pursued a degree in Computer Science.4 During my time at the university, I was able to develop discipline in studying on my own, making new discoveries, and forming new skills over time.5 The pandemic pushes us in ways that we are not familiar with. 6 Over the last few years, people are adapting to new terms and societal approaches. 7 The education system proposed a method of distance learning so students can be able to continue studying in the middle of the pandemic. 8 The first year of college wasn't so welcoming at all. The fact that I cannot meet or even recognize the names and faces of my classmates is indeed a source of sheer misery. 9 Speaking candidly, there are moments when I feel as though I cannot become a professional after pursuing the use of such a novel educational system because it doesn't genuinely feel like I'm in college. 10 Poor execution of the school system provided me with a responsibility to discipline myself. 11 With that in mind, I made the decision to step up my game and enroll in a more prestigious university in the hopes that they could help me train and develop my skills to become a professional. 12 I transferred to a university called The University of the East. It was a fresh start, knowing and experiencing a good sudden change of environment. 13 With knowledge of and exposure to a positive abrupt environmental change, it was a new beginning. 14 As of now, we are having a combination of a face to face and online learning. 15 I'm trying to be the version of myself by gradually practicing good habits that surely help me to enhance my skills and knowledge. 16 At the moment, I am learning the ways of living by myself. 17 I'm currently learning how to live independently. 18 College is fun, with classmates and friends until you experience having a tres, gosh it gives me mental stress then your going to be depressed. 19 I'm currently modifying and honing strategies to deal with school difficulties. 20 I am hoping or rather I say planning to walk on a bright path ahead.
